Chapter 01 — Definition

What is a Customer Journey Map?

A visual story of every step a person takes with your brand — from the first time they hear about you, to the day they recommend you to a friend.

Every CJM answers three questions:

📍

Where?

Touchpoints — channels they use

⚡

What?

Actions — what they do

💭

How?

Emotions — how they feel

Chapter 03 — Structure

The anatomy of a journey map

Every good CJM has 6 layers stacked along the customer's path. Without these, it's just a flowchart.

📱

Channel

Where they meet you — Instagram, email, store

🎯

Action

What they do — click, sign up, watch, buy

💭

Thought

What they're thinking — questions, doubts, hopes

😊

Emotion

How they feel — curious, frustrated, proud

❌

Pain point

What stops or annoys them — the friction

✅

Opportunity

How you can fix it — the action item

STAGE 2

STAGE 3

STAGE 4
💳

STAGE 5
❤️
Loyalty

In the next 5 slides, we'll go deep into each stage — what to map, what customers feel, and how to spot opportunities.

Stage 01 of 05 — 👀 Awareness

"Wait... what is this?"

The customer becomes aware your brand exists. They don't know what you do yet — they only know you exist.

Common channels
Ads · Social · SEO · Word of mouth · PR
Customer thought
"What is this? Is it real?"
What to map
Hook quality, channel mix, first 5 secondsWhat grabs them — and what scrolls past
Key metric
Reach · CTR · Cost per click
🤔
Typical emotion: Curious + skeptical
Stage 02 of 05 — 🤔 Consideration

"Could this work for me?"

They engage deeper. They compare you with others. They look for proof you're not a scam and that you can actually solve their problem.

Common channels
Website · Reviews · Demo · Free trial · Comparison pages
Customer thought
"Looks interesting. But is it really for me?"
What to map
Trust signals, social proof, clarity of valueHow quickly do they "get" what you do?
Key metric
Time on site · Demo signups · Engagement
😮
Typical emotion: Interested + still doubting
Stage 03 of 05 — 💪 Decision

"I think this is the one"

The customer commits internally. They've narrowed the choice. Now they're looking for the final reasons to pull the trigger — or to back out.

Common channels
Pricing page · Sales call · Testimonials · Final FAQ
Customer thought
"Almost ready. Just need one more reason."
What to map
Decision drivers, objections, hesitation pointsWhat stops them at the very last second?
Key metric
Pricing-page conversion · Cart-add rate
💪
Typical emotion: Confident — but anxious
Stage 04 of 05 — 💳 Action

"Take my money"

The customer buys, signs up, books. Most companies celebrate here. This is also where the most preventable money is lost.

Common channels
Checkout · Signup form · Payment · Confirmation
Customer thought
"OK, doing it. Please don't break."
What to map
Form friction, payment errors, abandonmentEvery extra field costs you customers
Key metric
Checkout completion · Payment success rate
🎉
Typical emotion: Excited + slightly nervous
Stage 05 of 05 — ❤️ Loyalty

From customer to ambassador

The journey doesn't end at "buy". A loyal customer comes back, spends more, and brings friends. This is where the real revenue lives.

Common channels
Email · App · Community · Referral · Support
Customer thought
"This was good. I should tell someone."
What to map
Repeat use, NPS, referral triggers, churn riskLoyalty is built — or broken — in the first 30 days
Key metric
Retention · NPS · Referrals · Lifetime value
❤️
Typical emotion: Belonging + pride
Chapter 05 — Practice

How to build one — 5 steps

1

Pick a real persona

One specific customer. Not "everyone".

2

List every touchpoint

Walk through the journey from their eyes — every channel they hit.

3

Talk to real customers

5 interviews beat 50 assumptions. Ask: where did you struggle?

4

Map emotions + pain points

Mark each step with the feeling and the friction.

5

Find quick wins

Pick the 3 biggest frictions. Fix one this week.

Chapter 06 — Take-aways

3 things to remember

01

Map = compass

You can't improve what you can't see. The CJM makes the invisible visible.

02

Emotions beat features

People remember how you made them feel — not your feature list.

03

A CJM is never finished

Customers change. Channels change. Your map should change too.
